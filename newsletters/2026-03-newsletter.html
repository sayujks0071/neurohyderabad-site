💡 3 Signs You Should See a Neurosurgeon (Not Just a Physiotherapist)

  1. Leg weakness or foot drop — If you're tripping, dragging your foot, or can't stand on your toes, this suggests nerve compression that needs urgent evaluation.
  2. Loss of bladder or bowel control — This is an emergency (cauda equina syndrome). Go to the ER immediately.
  3. Pain that hasn't improved after 6 weeks of physiotherapy — Persistent sciatica despite proper conservative treatment may indicate a structural problem that needs surgical assessment.
